What is Zakat?

Zakat is an obligatory form of charity in Islam, meaning “purification” and “growth.” Every Muslim who owns wealth above a specific threshold (Nisab) must pay 2.5% of their surplus assets to those in need.

📌 Nisab concept (Silver basis)

The Nisab threshold is the minimum amount of wealth a Muslim must possess before Zakat becomes due. Our calculator follows the silver Nisab (approx. 612 grams of silver). If your net assets are greater than or equal to the current silver value you enter, Zakat is payable.

👤 Who must pay Zakat?

Any adult Muslim (sane, free) who has complete ownership of wealth equal to or exceeding the Nisab, and one lunar year has passed (hawl), is liable. This tool helps you compute the amount, but always consult your local scholar for personal rulings.
