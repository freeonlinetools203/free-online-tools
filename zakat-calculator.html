What is Zakat and Why It Matters?

Zakat is one of the five pillars of Islam and an obligatory form of charity for every eligible Muslim. It means "purification" and "growth" – purifying your wealth and blessing what remains. Every Muslim who possesses wealth above the Nisab threshold must pay 2.5% of their surplus assets to those in need. Our Zakat calculator helps you determine your exact Zakat obligation quickly and accurately.

Zakat = 2.5% × Net Wealth

Net Wealth = Total Assets − Total Debts

What is Nisab and Why It Matters?

Nisab is the minimum amount of wealth a Muslim must possess before Zakat becomes obligatory. There are two Nisab thresholds based on Islamic tradition:

  • Gold Nisab: Approximately 87.48 grams of gold
  • Silver Nisab: Approximately 612.36 grams of silver

Most scholars recommend using the silver Nisab as it benefits more people. If your net wealth equals or exceeds the Nisab value, Zakat is due at 2.5%.

What Assets Are Subject to Zakat?

💰 Cash

Cash in hand and bank accounts

✨ Gold & Silver

All forms of gold and silver jewelry

📈 Investments

Stocks, shares, and business assets

🏪 Business Inventory

Trading goods and merchandise

🏦 Savings

Retirement funds and savings accounts

💰 Loans Given

Money lent to others (if recoverable)

What Assets Are Exempt from Zakat?

  • 🏠 Primary Residence: The home you live in
  • 🚗 Personal Vehicles: Cars used for personal transportation
  • 👕 Personal Belongings: Clothes, furniture, household items
  • 📚 Personal Items: Books, electronics for personal use
  • 💼 Business Equipment: Tools and machinery used for work

Who Should Receive Zakat?

  • 🔹 Al-Fuqara (The Poor): Those who have little to no wealth
  • 🔹 Al-Masakin (The Needy): Those in severe poverty
  • 🔹 Amil (Zakat Collectors): Those appointed to collect Zakat
  • 🔹 Muallaf (New Muslims): Those whose hearts need reconciliation
  • 🔹 Ar-Riqab (Slaves): To free slaves (historically)
  • 🔹 Al-Gharimin (Debtors): Those burdened by debt
  • 🔹 Fi Sabilillah (In Allah's Cause): For the cause of Islam
  • 🔹 Ibn as-Sabil (Travelers): Stranded travelers in need

❓ Frequently Asked Questions About Zakat

📌 What is Zakat?

Zakat is an obligatory form of charity in Islam, one of the five pillars. Every eligible Muslim must pay 2.5% of their surplus wealth to those in need.

📌 What is Nisab in Zakat?

Nisab is the minimum amount of wealth a Muslim must possess before Zakat becomes due. It is based on the value of gold (87.48g) or silver (612.36g).

📌 Is this Zakat Calculator free?

Yes, this Zakat calculator is completely free with unlimited use. No registration or payment required.

📌 Do I pay Zakat on gold jewelry?

Yes, Zakat is due on gold and silver jewelry if it reaches the Nisab threshold, regardless of whether it's for personal use or investment.

📌 Can I pay Zakat during Ramadan?

Yes, you can pay Zakat at any time of the year. However, many Muslims prefer to pay during Ramadan due to the increased rewards of good deeds.

📌 Does this calculator store my financial data?

No, all calculations happen locally in your browser. We do not store, track, or share any financial or personal information.

📌 What is the Zakat rate?

The Zakat rate is 2.5% (or 1/40) of your net wealth that has been in your possession for one lunar year.

📌 Who is eligible to receive Zakat?

Zakat can be given to eight categories: the poor, the needy, Zakat collectors, new Muslims, freeing slaves, debtors, for Allah's cause, and stranded travelers.
