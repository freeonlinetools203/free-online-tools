What is BMI?

BMI (Body Mass Index) is a simple calculation that uses your height and weight to estimate whether you have a healthy body weight.

BMI Formula

BMI = Weight (kg) ÷ Height (m²)

BMI Categories

Complete Guide to Body Mass Index (BMI)

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a widely used health measurement that helps determine whether a person has a healthy body weight based on height and weight. It is a quick and simple method used by healthcare professionals worldwide to screen for potential weight-related health risks.

BMI is calculated using the formula: BMI = Weight (kg) ÷ Height (m²). Although it does not directly measure body fat percentage, it provides a reliable estimate for most adults.

Why BMI Matters for Your Health

Maintaining a healthy BMI is important because excess body weight or being underweight can increase the risk of serious health problems.

  • Heart disease
  • High blood pressure
  • Type 2 diabetes
  • Joint problems
  • Sleep apnea
  • Certain cancers

By regularly checking your BMI, you can monitor your weight status and take early action if necessary.

Understanding BMI Categories

BMI results are generally classified into the following categories:

  • Underweight: BMI less than 18.5
  • Normal weight: BMI between 18.5 and 24.9
  • Overweight: BMI between 25 and 29.9
  • Obese: BMI 30 or higher

If your BMI falls outside the normal range, it does not necessarily mean you are unhealthy. However, it may indicate that further health evaluation is needed.

Limitations of BMI

While BMI is useful, it does have limitations:

  • It does not measure body fat percentage directly.
  • Muscular individuals may have a high BMI but low body fat.
  • It does not account for age, gender, or body composition differences.

For a complete health evaluation, BMI should be combined with other health assessments such as waist circumference, body fat percentage, and lifestyle analysis.

How to Maintain a Healthy BMI

Maintaining a healthy BMI involves adopting a balanced and sustainable lifestyle.

  • Eat a balanced diet rich in fruits and vegetables
  • Exercise regularly (at least 150 minutes per week)
  • Stay hydrated
  • Avoid excessive sugar and processed foods
  • Get enough sleep

Consistency is key when it comes to maintaining long-term health.
